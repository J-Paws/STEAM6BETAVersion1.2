It's Art

Why art is important to our developing society

“Art is a highly diverse range of human activities engaged in creating visual, auditory, or performed artifacts— artworks—that express the author’s imaginative or technical skill, and are intended to be appreciated for their beauty or emotional power."

Art, in its broadest sense, is a form of communication. It means whatever the artist intends it to mean, and this meaning is shaped by the materials, techniques, and forms it makes use of, as well as the ideas and feelings it creates in its viewers. Art is an act of expressing feelings, thoughts, and observations.

Imagine

It is the ability to produce and simulate novel objects, sensations, and ideas in the mind without any immediate input of the senses. Imagination helps make knowledge applicable in solving problems and is fundamental to integrating experience and the learning process.

Explore

To search for more ideas and creativity by imagining possible outcome that can solve solutions. We may look into new things during exploration for the purpose of discovery of resources or information.

Express

Through expression, we'll be able to explore emotions and at the same time create something beautiful of of them. Expression helps us to create something that comes from our imagination into something real.

Engage

It is a primal skill enabling us to express ourselves and connect to others in ways that surpass cultural, linguistic, and even psychological barriers.

Benefits of Arts

Art gives us the ability to express ourselves. Through that expression, we communicate by drawing on our own unique emotions, thoughts and experiences. When you see and study another's art, you're seeing the world through their eyes. When you create, you're letting the world see through yours.
